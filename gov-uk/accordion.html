Choosing a supplier

When you choose software, you start a new relationship with your chosen supplier. Understanding the benefits and limits of what you're buying will help to minimise unexoected surorises down the line.

  • Ask questions and only say yes if you're happy with the answers. Is the conversation filled with jargon you don't understand? If so, this may not be the supplier for you.
  • Check the software meets all of your needs.Does the software a supplier is offering meet your needs? Are there extras you'll be buying, but won't use? A study by the Standish Group revealed that only 29% of software rollouts are completed on time, on budget and meet user requirements.
  • Check the level of supplier support.Ask the supplier about the support that comes free and at extra charge.
